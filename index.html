Benjamin Faltesek Philosophy of mathematics and logic Chapman University program in Mathematics, Philosophy, and Physics ABOUT I am a philosopher of mathematics and logic. My current work concerns mathematical modality, particularly how possible constructions generate and transmit necessity. RESEARCH INTERESTS Mathematical modality Foundations of mathematics Semantic antirealism Formal epistemology TEACHING I have taught several philosophy courses as well as courses in symbolic and informal logic. Philosophy, logic, and mathematics are not to be reserved for a select few; as archetypes of human reasoning they must be available to everyone. INTERDISCIPLINARITY I specialize in philosophy but my program is interdisciplinary (mathematics, philosophy, physics). Interdisciplinary research is rewarding but challenging. Even sympathetic mathematicians want decisive solutions: a proof to demonstrate or a counterexample to refute. Philosophers offer novel solutions, sometimes illuminating ones, but then show how fundamental problems recur anyway. Philosophers and physicists thus share the mindset that no solution is final.
